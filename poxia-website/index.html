Fully Automatic Pathology Machines to Be Installed in Medical Colleges & District Hospitals by June 15

A presentation by Meril was held in the office of Additional Chief Secretary Ajay Kumar Singh, Dept. of Health, Medical Education & Family Welfare. The company demonstrated machines capable of processing 50 samples simultaneously, with a 10-year maintenance commitment. Reports will be delivered directly to patients via WhatsApp.

  • Phase 1: All medical colleges & district hospitals by June 15, 2026
  • Phase 2: Expansion to CHC and PHC level across Jharkhand
  • Full automation — urine & blood tests with instant WhatsApp reports
  • Software, machine operation & manpower training included

Udit Vani May 2026

Jharkhand Hospitals to Get Fully Automatic Pathology Machines — Launch in June

In a major push to modernize healthcare diagnostics, the Jharkhand state government has approved the deployment of fully automatic pathology machines across all government hospitals. The initiative — powered by Meril diagnostic technology and LIS software integration — aims to eliminate manual pathology workflows entirely.

  • State-wide deployment across Jharkhand's government hospital network
  • Replacing semi-automatic machines with fully automated analyzers
  • Digital report delivery via integrated LIS + WhatsApp pipeline
  • C-DAC HMIS integration for interoperability
